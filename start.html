Get Started — Your Practical Checklist

Ready to step into the world of sheepdog trialling? Whether you're a student, young handler, or complete beginner, start with easy observation, build connections, then gradually move into hands-on practice.

Observe and Learn

  • Visit local sheepdog trials — note timing, positioning and handler restraint.
  • Watch how sheep respond to pressure, terrain, gates and noise.
  • Speak to handlers after their run — most are welcoming and enjoy sharing advice.

Connect and Explore

Many communities now live online. Join Instagram tags like #sheepdogtrials, Facebook training groups, or structured platforms like Sheepdog School.

You can also contact your regional ISDS secretary for calendars, training days, and beginner-friendly events near you.

Begin Your Journey — Practical Options

  1. Attend a beginner session or introductory herding clinic.
  2. Rent a small field or collaborate with a local farmer for practice space.
  3. Volunteer at trials to learn judging, scribing, and stewarding roles.
  4. Document your progress — short clips and notes accelerate learning and build community support.

Assignment Checklist & Compliance

Your university submission must include a supplementary PDF containing:

  • All website text: copy-paste your site content cleanly into the PDF.
  • Copyright information: list each image/video, source, and license (Unsplash, Creative Commons, or your own work).
  • People & GUEP: any interviews, quotes or identifiable individuals require consent and GUEP approval.
  • AI assistance statement: declare if AI helped draft or proofread any element.
  • Academic Integrity Declaration: paste the required departmental form.

Tip: keep a small folder containing screenshots, links, licenses and signed forms — it prevents last-minute stress when submitting.
